How I Think
01
User-first, always
When people trust you with what matters to them, every interaction counts. A confusing flow doesn't just frustrate — it erodes trust entirely.
02
Systems over screens
I build foundations — design tokens connected to code, governance frameworks, service blueprints. Things that let teams move fast without breaking things.
03
Simplicity is the hardest work
Anyone can add complexity. The real craft is removing it — finding the clearest path through a complicated domain, making interfaces feel obvious.
04
Trust through craft
Visual polish, smooth interactions, thoughtful micro-moments — these aren't nice-to-haves. They're signals that say "you're in good hands."
05
Ship, then polish
I'd rather ship 80% and learn from real users than perfect in isolation. The best designs come from contact with reality, not longer review cycles.
06
Study what shipped
I study Wise, Airbnb, Stripe — real products with real rationale. Not concept shots. The best inspiration comes from what survived production.
Leadership
My role isn't to have all the answers. It's to ask the right questions, remove blockers, set the bar high, and get out of the way.

I believe in empowerment over micromanagement. The designers I've led — some now at Revolut, some building their own startups — will tell you: I trust my team to own their work.

I create systems so people can succeed independently. I push for craft culture while celebrating individual growth.

## Design Context

### Users
**Primary:** Hiring managers, CTOs, and startup founders in fintech/crypto evaluating a senior product designer. They scan portfolios late at night, comparing 10+ candidates. They need to feel "this person is serious" in 3 seconds.
**Secondary (consulting page):** Startup CEOs like early-stage founders who saw a LinkedIn post and want AI + design help. They're not designers — they need to understand what you do without jargon.

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** Obsidian. Precise. Alive.
- Voice: Confident, direct, never overselling. Says what it is, not what it aspires to be.
- Emotional goal: The visitor should feel they're looking at someone who operates at a higher level — not flashy, just unmistakably competent.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme:** Dark mode primary. Obsidian/volcanic brand language on homepage. Sub-pages are cleaner dark, no volcanic imagery.
- **Colors:** Void #060606, Obsidian #0a0a0a, Basalt #111110, Depth #0E0F10. Warm white #f5f0e8 (never pure white). Rose #e11d48 as sole accent.
- **Typography:** Tanker (display, ALWAYS ALL CAPS, large scale), General Sans (headings/UI), Literata (body, italic for supporting text). Self-hosted.
- **Icons:** Phosphor Bold only. No custom SVGs for icons, no emoji.
- **Motion:** Scroll-driven reveals, scenicDrift on images, tube-light flicker animation as brand signature. GSAP available but currently using CSS + vanilla JS.
- **Grid:** 40px desktop padding, 24px mobile, 1400px max-width centered containers.
- **References:** lusion.co and oryzo.ai as aspirational north stars. Editorial/magazine meets industrial precision.
- **Anti-references:** Generic SaaS landing pages, purple gradient AI aesthetic, Dribbble-style decoration without purpose, glassmorphism.

### Design Principles
1. **Every pixel earns its place.** No decoration for decoration's sake. If it doesn't serve the narrative, remove it.
2. **Typography IS the design.** Tanker at large scale creates visual weight. The text itself is the hero, not imagery layered behind it.
3. **Dark means depth, not flat.** Use radial glows, grain textures, subtle gradients to give the void dimension. Flat black is a loading screen.
4. **Interaction proves craft.** Hover states, scroll reveals, active feedback — these compound into "this person is a designer." Emil Kowalski's principles: ease-out for entries, 150-250ms for hover, scale(0.97) on active, never animate from scale(0).
5. **Consistency across pages.** Same grid, same padding, same font hierarchy, same interaction patterns. A visitor should feel they're in one cohesive world, not 5 different sites.

Merchant Onboarding

19 steps to become a merchant. Target: 100,000 stores. The math didn’t work. The onboarding had to change.

Lead Product Designer Service Design Ground Force iOS & Android

At 19 steps per merchant, 100,000 was fantasy. 0.3% activation. 63% churn after first order. The onboarding was designed for a pilot, not for scale.

The Scale Problem

19 steps. Ambassador required at every one.

  • 19-step onboarding across fragmented screens and flows
  • Phone-number-based linking — fragile, created broken records
  • English-only interface for merchants who may not read English
  • Complete ambassador dependency at every step
  • 0.3% activation — 573 onboarded, 2 transacting
  • 63% churn after first order

QR Self-Service

One QR code. Three lifecycle states.

Inspired by BharatPe — 13 million merchants, 4–5 step onboarding — I redesigned everything around a single artifact: a pre-printed QR code that routes the entire flow.

📦
STATE 1
Unassigned
Batch-generated. Sitting in inventory. Not linked to anyone.
🤝
STATE 2
Ambassador-Claimed
Ambassador picks it up. Pre-fills merchant name, location, photo.
🏪
STATE 3
Merchant-Assigned
Merchant scans. Onboarding complete. Transacting.

No phone numbers. No passwords. No manual linking. The QR code IS the authentication. The app detects which state it’s in and branches accordingly.

Key Decisions

What made it scale.

Immediate Role Grant
No approval queue. Scan, fill one screen, you’re a merchant. Bottleneck eliminated.
Unified Screen
Multiple onboarding screens consolidated into one Store Details submission. Ambassador pre-filled most of it.
Authless Architecture
No phone verification. No OTPs. The QR’s lifecycle states handle all routing and authentication.
First Sale = Completion
Ambassador becomes the first customer. Activation happens during onboarding, not “sometime later.”

Service Design

The ambassador is trust. The app is onboarding.

I mapped the full onboarding service — three swim lanes. The critical insight: the ambassador’s job ends when the QR is scanned. Everything after is self-service. Separating trust-building from flow execution is what makes this scale without linear headcount growth.

Learnings

What this taught me.

01
Onboarding IS the growth engine.
Every friction in onboarding is friction in the entire flywheel. 19 steps doesn’t slow one merchant — it caps the whole network.
02
Pre-fill creates magic.
When a merchant scans and sees their store already configured — name, photo, $5 — something shifts. Invisible work before creates the wow moment after.
03
First transaction during onboarding.
If the merchant leaves without transacting, 63% never come back. The ambassador as first customer ensures activation in the moment.
04
Design the system, not just the screen.
Ground Force — qualification, economics, anti-fraud, distribution — is as much a design problem as any UI. The flow only works because the system behind it works.

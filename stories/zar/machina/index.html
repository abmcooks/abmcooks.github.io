MACHINA — AI DESIGN FOR EVERYONE

An internal AI design suite that removes the design team bottleneck. 5 tools that let engineers and non-designers create design artifacts at speed.

Company ZAR Year 2025 Role Head of Design

THE CHALLENGE

The design team was a bottleneck. Engineers waited for designers to produce mockups, icons, illustrations, and UI specifications before they could build. In a startup moving fast, those waiting periods accumulated into weeks of lost velocity. Every "quick design request" was another item in an already full queue.

The traditional solution — hire more designers — doesn't scale at the speed a startup needs. And it doesn't address the root problem: the dependency itself. As long as design is a service that others request, the queue will always exist.

The question was radical: what if engineers could do design work themselves, with AI assistance that ensures quality and brand consistency?

60-80% faster iteration with AI-assisted design

THE APPROACH

I analyzed every type of design request that came to the team and categorized them by how much design judgment they required. Some tasks — like generating app store screenshots, creating social media graphics, or producing icon variants — followed patterns that could be systematized and assisted by AI.

The key insight was that AI doesn't replace design thinking — it replaces design production. A designer still defines the system, the rules, the brand. But the execution of those rules on routine tasks can be augmented by tools that understand the system.

I built MACHINA as a suite of 5 internal tools, each targeting a specific design production bottleneck. Each tool was trained on ZAR's brand guidelines, design tokens, and component library — so its outputs were brand-consistent by default.

The tools were designed to be used by non-designers. Clear interfaces, sensible defaults, and guardrails that prevent off-brand outputs. Engineers don't need to learn Figma — they need to describe what they want and get something usable.

THE SOLUTION

MACHINA is an internal AI design suite consisting of 5 specialized tools. Each tool handles a specific category of design production: UI mockup generation from text descriptions, icon and illustration creation within brand guidelines, marketing asset generation (app store, social media), design spec documentation from Figma files, and design review automation that flags inconsistencies.

The suite is 60-80% complete and already in active use within ZAR. Engineers use it daily for tasks that previously required a designer's involvement. The tools produce drafts that are good enough to ship for internal purposes and need only light design review for customer-facing outputs.

MACHINA is being prepared for commercialization — the plan is to make it available as a standalone product for other companies facing the same design bottleneck challenge.

THE IMPACT

5 AI design tools
60-80% Faster iteration
0 Design bottleneck
Next Commercialization

MACHINA fundamentally changed ZAR's design operations. Engineers now handle routine design tasks with AI assistance, freeing the design team to focus on strategic work — user research, complex interaction design, and system-level thinking. The bottleneck didn't just shrink; the dependency model itself changed.

The broader implication is significant: design teams in startups don't need to scale linearly with engineering. With the right AI tools trained on the right design system, a small design team can support a much larger engineering organization without becoming a constraint.

THE JOURNEY

When I joined SadaPay in December 2020, there was no app, no design team, and no design system. Just a bold idea — build Pakistan's first neobank — and a CEO who believed design would be the difference.

I was the founding designer. The first screen, the first flow, the first pixel — all mine. Within months, I'd designed the entire mobile banking experience from scratch: onboarding, KYC, wallets, transfers, bill payments, card management, and the gamified waitlist that would change everything.

The waitlist was the breakout moment. 700,000 people signed up with zero paid acquisition. I designed a gamification system — Founders Club cards, golden tickets, branded merchandise, a referral engine. Celebrities felt FOMO. Irfan Junejo came to the office. It was the first time a Pakistani fintech had created genuine cultural hype. The design of the waitlist experience became a case study in viral product design.

From there, I built the team. I hired 15-20 designers over my tenure, managing a core team of 8 at peak. Associates straight from university with messy portfolios. I saw something in them. The joke in Pakistan's design community became: "Getting into Harvard is easier than getting into SadaPay's design team."

I challenged what everyone accepted. I read the State Bank of Pakistan's regulations myself — because the PM told me "that's just how it works" and I don't accept that answer. I proposed removing unnecessary PINs and OTPs, introducing biometric-only login, putting emojis in a banking app (a first in Pakistan), and submitting a QR code payment proposal to SBP years before the industry adopted it.

I built Nukta — SadaPay's design system with design tokens. It was the first token-based design system in Pakistan's tech industry, built in 2021-2022 before design tokens were mainstream. Nukta gave us consistency at scale and gave the engineers a shared language with design.

I designed SadaBiz, the business banking product for freelancers that processed $1B+ in annualized volume. Apple Pay and Google Pay integration. Service design blueprints that the CEO called my "superpower" — they helped reduce fraud by mapping every touchpoint in the system.

SadaPay helped raise $7.2M pre-seed (the largest by a Pakistani startup at the time), followed by seed and Series A — totaling $20M+. The design was a selling point in every investor deck. In 2024, Papara acquired SadaPay for $50M.

I was laid off post-acquisition. Zero compensation. Stocks worth nothing. But SadaPay changed Pakistan's fintech landscape permanently — every bank revamped their app after us. And the designers I mentored are now leading teams at Revolut, and companies across the world.

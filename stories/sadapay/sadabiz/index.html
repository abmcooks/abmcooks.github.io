SADABIZ — $1B+ FOR PAKISTAN'S FREELANCERS

Designing business banking for a new generation of Pakistani freelancers. Apple Pay, Google Pay, international transfers, and service design blueprints that reduced fraud.

Company SadaPay Year 2022 – 2024 Role Head of Product Design

THE CHALLENGE

Pakistan has one of the world's fastest-growing freelance economies, but its banking infrastructure was built for a different era. Freelancers earning from international platforms like Upwork, Fiverr, and Toptal had no clean way to receive payments, manage business finances, or separate personal and business spending. Existing banks treated them like afterthoughts.

SadaPay saw the opportunity: build a business banking product specifically for this underserved segment. But the challenge went deeper than UI. The entire service journey — from international payment receipt to local currency conversion to business expense management — needed to be mapped, understood, and designed end-to-end.

This wasn't just a new feature. It was a new product with new users, new regulatory requirements, and new technical integrations including Apple Pay and Google Pay.

$1B+ annualized volume through SadaBiz

THE APPROACH

I led with service design blueprints — detailed maps of every touchpoint, every system interaction, every human handoff in the SadaBiz journey. Front-stage (what users see), back-stage (what operations handles), and support processes (what happens when things go wrong). The CEO called these blueprints my "superpower."

The blueprints revealed critical friction points and fraud vectors that weren't visible from a screen-level view. By mapping the complete service, we could identify where users dropped off, where manual intervention was needed, and where bad actors could exploit gaps in the system.

For Apple Pay and Google Pay integration, I worked directly with engineering to understand the technical constraints and designed flows that met both platform guidelines and SadaPay's brand standards. The card provisioning experience needed to feel seamless — one tap to add your SadaPay card to your phone's wallet.

User research with Pakistani freelancers informed every decision. Their pain points were specific: delayed international transfers, confusing currency conversion, no way to generate invoices, and the constant friction of switching between personal and business accounts.

THE SOLUTION

SadaBiz launched as a comprehensive business banking product for freelancers. The core experience included international payment receipt with competitive FX rates, instant notifications and transaction categorization, business expense management with receipt capture, Apple Pay and Google Pay integration for instant card access, and invoice generation tied to the payment flow.

The UI was designed to feel like an extension of the SadaPay personal app — familiar enough to be intuitive, distinct enough to signal "this is your business." The account switching experience was a single swipe, keeping both worlds accessible without confusion.

The service design blueprints didn't just inform the product — they became operational tools. The operations team used them to train new staff, and the fraud team used them to identify and close vulnerability windows.

THE IMPACT

$1B+ Annualized volume
40% Fraud reduction
2 Pay integrations
E2E Service blueprints

SadaBiz processed over $1 billion in annualized volume, making it one of SadaPay's most important revenue drivers. The service design blueprints directly contributed to fraud reduction by exposing and closing system-level vulnerabilities that screen-level design would never catch.

The Apple Pay and Google Pay integrations made SadaPay one of the first Pakistani fintechs to offer tap-to-pay through mobile wallets. For freelancers, SadaBiz became the banking product they'd been waiting for — purpose-built for how they actually work.

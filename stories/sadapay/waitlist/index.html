THE WAITLIST THAT BROKE PAKISTAN'S INTERNET

How gamification, golden tickets, and zero ad spend drove 700K sign-ups and made SadaPay a cultural moment before the app even launched.

Company SadaPay Year 2021 Role Founding Product Designer

THE CHALLENGE

SadaPay was building Pakistan's first neobank, but the app wasn't ready yet. The company had zero marketing budget, no brand recognition, and was entering a market where digital banking was barely a concept. The challenge was simple but enormous: how do you build hype for a product nobody can use yet?

Traditional fintech launches rely on paid acquisition — performance marketing, influencer deals, billboard campaigns. SadaPay had none of that. What we had was a bold visual identity, a product vision people could believe in, and the willingness to try something nobody in Pakistan's tech scene had attempted before.

The question became: could we make people desperate to join something that didn't exist yet?

700K sign-ups, zero ad spend

THE APPROACH

I designed a multi-layered gamification system inspired by the psychology of exclusivity and social proof. The core mechanic was simple: sign up, get a position in the queue, move up by referring friends. But the details made it addictive.

Founders Club tiers gave early adopters status. The higher your tier, the more exclusive your card design, the earlier your access. I designed distinct visual identities for each tier — from standard to gold to the ultra-rare platinum — making each one feel like a collectible.

Golden tickets were the viral engine. Randomly distributed, they let holders skip the entire queue. The unpredictability created a lottery-like excitement. People shared screenshots of their golden tickets on social media, which drove more sign-ups organically.

I also designed branded merchandise — exclusive to top referrers — turning the waitlist into a status symbol. Hoodies, stickers, card skins. Each piece reinforced the brand and gave people something physical to show off.

THE SOLUTION

The final waitlist experience was a complete product in itself. Users landed on a page that felt premium — dark, bold, with the SadaPay brand front and center. After signing up, they entered a gamified dashboard showing their queue position, referral count, tier status, and what they'd unlock next.

The referral sharing was designed to be frictionless. One tap to copy a link, with pre-written messages optimized for WhatsApp (Pakistan's dominant messaging platform). Every share felt personal, not spammy.

The leaderboard created healthy competition. Top referrers were visible, driving people to recruit harder. And the merchandise drops — announced with countdown timers — created urgency that kept people coming back.

THE IMPACT

700K+ Total sign-ups
$0 Paid acquisition
100% Organic growth
#1 Cultural moment

The waitlist became more than a growth hack — it became a cultural phenomenon. Celebrities joined organically. Irfan Junejo, one of Pakistan's biggest YouTubers, visited the office unprompted. People posted their queue positions on Instagram stories. SadaPay became the first Pakistani fintech to generate genuine FOMO.

The design of the waitlist experience set the tone for everything that followed. It proved that in a market with no precedent for digital banking hype, the right combination of design, psychology, and brand could create demand out of thin air.

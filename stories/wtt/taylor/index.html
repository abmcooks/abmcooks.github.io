THE TAYLOR ASSESSMENT — MEASURING CULTURE AT SCALE

Redesigning the world's only psychometric tool that measures individual contribution to organizational culture. 30 behaviors, 6 archetypes, 30 minutes.

Company Walking the Talk Year 2023 – 2024 Role Senior Product Designer

THE CHALLENGE

Walking the Talk's Taylor Assessment is unique in the market — the only psychometric tool that measures how an individual contributes to organizational culture. It evaluates 30 behaviors across 6 culture archetypes (Achievement, Customer-Centric, One Team, Innovative, People First, and Greater Good). But the existing tool was showing its age.

The assessment was built for a pre-digital world. The experience was functional but uninspiring — long form pages, minimal feedback during the assessment, and results that required expert interpretation. In a world where leaders expect Spotify-level UX from every tool they use, the Taylor Assessment needed to meet modern expectations without compromising its psychometric rigor.

The challenge was delicate: redesign the experience for a digital-first world while preserving the scientific integrity that makes the tool credible. Every design decision had to be validated against the psychometric model — you can't optimize UX at the expense of assessment accuracy.

50,000+ leaders assessed across 200+ organizations

THE APPROACH

I worked closely with Walking the Talk's psychometric experts and the Psynet platform team to understand the constraints. Every question's presentation, ordering, and response mechanism had been calibrated over years of research. Changes to these elements required psychometric validation.

Within those constraints, there was significant room for improvement. The visual design, progress feedback, transition animations, results presentation, and overall flow could all be modernized without touching the psychometric core.

I designed the assessment as a journey, not a form. Each section (corresponding to a culture archetype) had its own visual identity within a cohesive whole. Progress was communicated through both explicit indicators and subtle visual cues. Transitions between sections felt like moving through chapters of a story about yourself.

The results experience was completely reimagined. Instead of dense data tables, I designed visual profiles that communicated culture contribution patterns at a glance — while still providing the depth that coaches and consultants needed for interpretation.

THE SOLUTION

The enhanced Taylor Assessment is a 30-minute online experience that feels closer to a personal discovery tool than a corporate psychometric test. The assessment flow guides users through 30 behaviors organized by culture archetype, with clear progress tracking and engaging micro-interactions that maintain attention without biasing responses.

The results dashboard presents individual culture profiles visually — showing where a leader's behaviors cluster across the six archetypes. Team and organization-level views aggregate individual data into culture heat maps that reveal patterns invisible at the individual level.

Built on the Psynet psychometric platform, the tool was press-released globally in January 2024 and adopted by organizations ranging from multinational corporations to government agencies.

THE IMPACT

The redesigned Taylor Assessment maintained its position as the only culture-contribution measurement tool on the market while dramatically improving the user experience. The January 2024 global press release marked a significant milestone for Walking the Talk's digital transformation.

The design proved that psychometric rigor and modern UX aren't in conflict — they're complementary. A better experience leads to more thoughtful responses, which leads to more accurate assessments, which leads to better organizational insights. Good design makes the science work better.
